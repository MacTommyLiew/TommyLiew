Introduction

The Pokémon Trading Card Game (TCG) is a two-player strategic card game where players build a deck of 60 cards and battle using Pokémon. Players take turns drawing cards, playing Pokémon onto the field, attaching Energy cards to power up attacks, and using Trainer cards for support. The goal is to knock out your opponent's Pokémon and collect all 6 Prize Cards before they collect yours.

Game Setup

  1. Each player must have a deck of exactly 60 cards.
  2. Both players shuffle their own decks. Either player may also shuffle the opponent's deck.
  3. Flip a coin or roll a die to decide who goes first.
  4. Each player draws 7 cards as their starting hand.
  5. If you have no Basic Pokémon in your hand, reveal your hand, shuffle it back, and redraw. This is called a mulligan. Your opponent may draw 1 extra card each time you mulligan.
  6. Place 1 Basic Pokémon face-down as your Active Pokémon.
  7. Place up to 5 more Basic Pokémon face-down on your Bench.
  8. Each player sets the top 6 cards of their deck face-down as Prize Cards.
  9. Flip all Pokémon face-up at the same time. The game begins!

Note: The first player does NOT draw a card on their first turn.

Basic Steps (Each Turn)

  1. Draw a card from the top of your deck.
  2. Play Basic Pokémon from your hand onto your Bench (optional).
  3. Evolve Pokémon that have been in play since your last turn (optional).
  4. Attach 1 Energy card from your hand to any of your Pokémon (once per turn).
  5. Play Trainer cards: use Item cards freely, play 1 Supporter card, or play a Stadium card (optional).
  6. Retreat your Active Pokémon by paying its Retreat Cost (optional, once per turn).
  7. Attack using your Active Pokémon. This ends your turn.

Note: Steps 2–6 can be done in any order. Attacking always ends your turn.

How to Win

A player wins by achieving any one of the following:

🏆

Collect all 6 Prize Cards

Take a Prize Card each time you knock out an opponent's Pokémon. Special rule Pokémon (ex, V) give 2 Prize Cards when knocked out.

❌

Opponent has no Pokémon left

If your opponent has no Active Pokémon and no Pokémon on the Bench, you win immediately.

📄

Opponent cannot draw a card

If your opponent's deck is empty and they cannot draw at the start of their turn, you win.

Tips for Beginners

🪙 Start with a Theme Deck

Pre-built decks are balanced and ready to play — perfect for learning the basics.

⚡ Balance Your Energy

Aim for 12–15 Basic Energy cards in your 60-card deck to avoid running out mid-game.

📘 Use Supporter Cards

Cards like Professor's Research let you draw 7 new cards. Use them when your hand is low.

🏆 Track Prize Cards

Always keep count of both players' Prize Cards. Knowing who is close to winning changes your strategy.

🔥 Use Type Advantage

Weakness doubles damage. If the opponent's Pokémon is weak to your type, prioritize those attacks.

💪 Bench Carefully

Don't bench Pokémon you don't need — some attacks hit the Bench, and a knocked-out Benched Pokémon still gives a Prize Card.
